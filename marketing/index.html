16 Things You Must Know Before Writing Any Copy ›
  1. No em dashes. Ever. Use commas, periods, or parentheses. This is a hard brand rule.
  2. CTA is "Check Availability" or "Book a Consultation." Never "Contact Us," never "Get a Quote."
  3. Don't compete on price. TM Weddings is premium. Do not run "affordable wedding photographer" ad copy.
  4. Lead with story, not specs. The brand's own copy never opens with "8-hour packages." It opens with "your wedding day deserves..."
  5. Faith messaging is powerful but segmented. Include it for values-aligned audiences. Don't force it into every campaign.
  6. The phrase "husband and wife" carries enormous emotional weight. Use it in copy, especially for cold audiences.
  7. Photo + film as one team is THE differentiator. Anchor most campaigns on this.
  8. Squarespace platform constraint. No new landing pages can be spun up without involving the existing dev workflow (code injection, header injection). Brief Tony on landing page needs early.
  9. Brand colors and fonts are non-negotiable. All static and motion creative must use #F6FEFE / #4aacac / #0f0f0f / Cormorant Garamond / Jost.
  10. Tony and Brynn approve all creative before it goes live. Build approval cycles into the campaign timeline.
